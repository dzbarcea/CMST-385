  • Quadeca Teams Up With brakence For New Song "A LA CARTE"

    Source: Ken Partridge, Genius News

    Earlier this month, the singer, rapper, songwriter, and YouTuber known as Quadeca announced his “SCRAPYARD SERIES,” wherein he’ll release five two-song collections, or packs, over the span of a few months—one every two or three weeks. Each song associated with this project will supposedly represent a different style, and yesterday brought the first pack, aptly titled SCRAPYARD I. The leadoff song, “A LA CARTE,” a collaboration with the electronic artist brakence, debuted inside the Top 50 of the Genius Top Songs chart.

  • The extremely online psychedelia of brakence

    Source: Mano Sundaresan, NPR

    Around three years ago, a teen pop artist from Ohio named brakence started to experience a relentless, nagging headache that lasted for months. This chronic pain, and brakence's broader understandings of his mind, body and environment, inspired his second album, the meticulous hypochondriac, released in December 2022 on Columbia Records. Raised in the suburbs of Columbus, brakence enjoyed a comfortable middle class upbringing that allowed him to explore his creative tendencies from an early age. He developed his voice in choir and his knowledge of music theory in jazz piano lessons. He attended an alternative middle school that abolished grades and sent him on "discovery days" to a family friend's basement studio to record. Around that time, he sunk deeper into the internet and grew obsessed with maximal, early 2010s dubstep.
